We live in a world that is constantly changing, and education must adapt to support students to be successful in this world. As an educator, it is my job to determine the needs of my students and support them in using their strengths to meet the challenges they face. I must be prepared to adapt my teaching practices, modify the learning environment, or provide individual accommodations to ensure learner growth. All students have the right to an education that meets their unique needs, which are as diverse and varied as each learner. My experience as an adjunct professor and elementary teacher has allowed me to learn about two very diverse groups of learners.

Adult learners bring their prior life experiences into their learning. Some have sailed through school with academic success, while others have faced challenges and may require extra support to achieve their goals. The students in my web development course have ranged from 20ish computer-savvy students to older adults with little to no computer skills. As this is a skill-based course, all students must complete the exact requirements, but my expectations and support differ. The first student group often moves ahead quickly and asks for support to make their website function more advanced. I can often use videos and links to support their learning, but I must also remind them to check the rubric for requirements. The second group needs one-on-one support. They need exemplars and individual, step-by-step guidance to understand how coding works to create a website that meets the specific course requirements. Both student groups have the right to personal growth and skill development; my job is to adjust my teaching interactions to meet those needs.

Elementary students, on the other hand, are at the beginning of their academic journey. It is a phase where they explore their strengths and passions. My role is to facilitate their growth and instill confidence in their abilities. I believe in their potential and constantly reinforce it. I aim to build strong relationships with my students so they feel comfortable taking the necessary risks for their development. I model expectations and provide scaffolding and support for students as they learn new skills. I modify the curriculum or provide accommodations to ensure every student succeeds. When students leave my classroom, they know they can and will learn.

>Regardless of age or background, every student deserves an education tailored to their unique needs. As an educator to future teachers, there is an additional responsibility. I must meet the needs of my teacher learners, but I must also prepare them for their future learners. I need to ensure my learners are prepared to meet the diverse needs of their students. A role I am passionate about embracing. To achieve this goal, I am committed to providing a supportive and inclusive learning environment that empowers my learners to succeed in a rapidly changing world.

My career as a teacher has spanned almost thirty years with a varitey of different roles and responsibilities.

Elementary Teacher

I was an elementary teacher in the Anchorage School District for 28 years. I taught kindergarten - 2nd grade at 5 different schools, in 9 different classrooms and with over 500 different students. My responsibilities extended past the classroom.

  • Building Technology Coordinator
  • Math Site-Based Specialist
  • Mentor Teacher
  • 504 Coordinator
  • SEL committee Member
  • Math Academic Alignment Leader
  • Host Teacher

While my biggest accomplishment was the difference I made in the lives of my students and colleagues, I am proud of being selected as the Presidential Award Winner for Excellent in Teaching Mathematics. It was an honor that truly changed my life.

Mathematics Researcher

My desire to become a researcher developed while working on my doctorate at the Unviersity of South Carolina. I was at the end of my elementary teaching experience, but still wanted to make a difference in education. My dissertation focused on the need for professional development to improved mathematics education. I studied the amount, quality and kind of professional development needed to impact teaching practices. This is something I would love to contine to research in hopes of making a significant change in how we support teachers.

Adjunct Professor

As an adjunct professor at the University of Missouri, I have had the opportunity to teach a variety of different courses in the School of Information Science & Learning Technologies Deparment. I have taught courses on how to use different LMS systems as well as Web Design and Development. Teaching college takes a different skillset and I have enjoyed learning how to connect with and support the adult learner.
